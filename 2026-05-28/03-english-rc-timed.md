# English — Reading Comprehension (Timed) — Day 8

> **સૂચના (Gujarati):** દરેક passage માટે timer લગાવો. પહેલા passage વાંચો, પછી પ્રશ્નો.

**Content:** English · **Time:** 45 min

---

## Passage 1 (12 min)

Digital banking has changed customer behavior in India. Many transactions that were previously done at branches are now completed through mobile applications and internet banking portals. This has reduced waiting time and improved convenience, especially in urban areas. However, increased digital adoption has also raised concerns about cybersecurity and fraud. Banks now invest in multi-factor authentication, real-time monitoring, and customer awareness campaigns. For candidates preparing for banking exams, understanding this shift is important because it affects both job roles and exam questions.

### Questions
1. What is a key benefit of digital banking mentioned?  
2. What is a major concern due to digital adoption?  
3. Why should exam aspirants understand this shift?  
4. Which area is specifically mentioned as benefiting more?  
5. True/False: Digital banking removed all need for branches.

---

## Passage 2 (12 min)

Time management is one of the most decisive factors in competitive exams. Many candidates know the concepts but lose marks because they spend too long on difficult questions. A balanced strategy includes identifying easy questions first, keeping strict sectional timing, and avoiding emotional decisions during the exam. Mock tests help candidates build this discipline. The goal of a mock is not only to score high but also to improve question selection and accuracy under pressure.

### Questions
1. Why do conceptually strong candidates still lose marks?  
2. What should be done first according to the passage?  
3. What does the passage call an emotional mistake?  
4. What are mocks useful for besides score?  
5. Choose the best title.

---

## Answer Key

Passage 1:  
1) Reduced waiting time / convenience  
2) Cybersecurity and fraud  
3) It affects banking roles and exam content  
4) Urban areas  
5) False

Passage 2:  
1) They spend too long on difficult questions  
2) Identify easy questions first  
3) Spending too much time on hard questions / panic decisions  
4) Improve selection + accuracy under pressure  
5) “Time Management in Competitive Exams” (or similar)
