# English — Nouns & Articles + Error Spotting (Day 1)

**Time:** 30 min · **Part A:** 15 min read · **Part B:** 15 questions · **Part C:** answers

---

## Part A — Grammar you need today

### Nouns

**Common noun:** general name (bank, officer, computer)  
**Proper noun:** specific name (RBI, India, Monday) — **capital letter**  
**Collective noun:** group (team, committee, jury) — verb singular or plural by context  
**Abstract noun:** idea (honesty, poverty, information) — often **uncountable**

**Countable vs uncountable**

| Countable | Uncountable (no plural) |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| books, errors | information, advice, furniture |
| many books | much information |
| fewer books | less information (wrong for count!) |

**Important:** *Information, equipment, furniture, advice* → **no plural** (not "informations")

**Possessive:**  
- Singular: add **'s** → the **officer's** desk  
- Plural ending -s: add **'** only → the **officers'** desk  

---

### Articles — a, an, the

| Article | Use |
|---------|-----|
| **a** | one general thing; consonant sound: **a** bank, **a** one-rupee note |
| **an** | vowel sound: **an** officer, **an** hour (silent h) |
| **the** | specific / already known / only one: **the** RBI, **the** Sun |

**No article (zero):**  
- General plural: **Computers** are useful.  
- Abstract general: **Honesty** is important.  
- Meals, languages: He speaks **English**.

**Common exam traps**
- Wrong: He is **best** student. → Right: He is **the best** student.  
- Wrong: **A** RBI governor → Right: **The** RBI governor (unique role in context)  
- Wrong: an **university** → Right: a **university** (sounds like "yu")  

---

### Error spotting method (5 steps)

1. Read the full sentence.  
2. Check **subject–verb agreement** (He **go** → He **goes**).  
3. Check **article** (a/an/the).  
4. Check **noun form** (count/uncount, plural).  
5. Compare with options — often only one grammar point is wrong.

## Part B — 15 error-spotting questions

*Each sentence has **one** error. Find the wrong part (A/B/C/D) or the wrong word. Write A, B, C, or D.*

**Format:**  
(A) part 1 | (B) part 2 | (C) part 3 | (D) part 4 — **one part has the error**

1. The manager ordered (A) his team to submit (B) their reports before (C) the deadline expires. (D) No error  
2. (A) An one-time password is sent (B) to your registered (C) mobile number. (D) No error  
3. She gave me (A) many advices on (B) how to prepare (C) for the interview. (D) No error  
4. (A) The informations shared by (B) the customer were (C) incorrect. (D) No error  
5. (A) A honest officer (B) refused the gift (C) from the vendor. (D) No error  
6. Each of the students (A) have completed (B) their assignment (C) on time. (D) No error  
7. (A) RBI has announced (B) a new guideline for (C) the rural banks. (D) No error  
8. He is (A) the best player in (B) our department and (C) everyone respect him. (D) No error  
9. (A) There is many reasons (B) for the delay in (C) processing the loan. (D) No error  
10. (A) The news about the merger (B) are spreading (C) across the branch. (D) No error  
11. (A) She bought (B) a umbrella because (C) it was raining. (D) No error  
12. (A) Equipments in the data centre (B) need regular (C) maintenance. (D) No error  
13. (A) The committee have decided (B) to approve (C) the IT budget. (D) No error  
14. (A) He works in (B) the State Bank of India since (C) 2022. (D) No error  
15. (A) One of my friend (B) works as (C) an IT officer. (D) No error

## Part C — Answers & short explanations

| Q | Error in | Fix |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | D | No error (sentence is correct) |
| 2 | A | **A** one-time → **A** one-time (use **a** one-time — "w" consonant sound) |
| 3 | B | **advices** → **advice** (uncountable) |
| 4 | A | **informations** → **information** |
| 5 | A | **A honest** → **An honest** (vowel sound) |
| 6 | A | **have** → **has** (Each → singular) |
| 7 | D | No error (acceptable in exam-style notice English) |
| 8 | C | **respect** → **respects** (everyone → singular verb) |
| 9 | A | **is many** → **are many** (reasons = plural) |
| 10 | B | **are** → **is** (news = singular) |
| 11 | B | **a umbrella** → **an umbrella** |
| 12 | A | **Equipments** → **Equipment** |
| 13 | A | **have** → **has** (committee as unit) |
| 14 | B | **in** → **at** OR **works** → **has worked** (since → present perfect: **has worked at**) |
| 15 | A | **friend** → **friends** (One of + plural) |

**Best answer for Q14:** **B** — "works … since 2022" → **has worked at** the State Bank…

**Score:** 12+ = strong · 9–11 = revise Part A · below 9 = re-read nouns/articles tomorrow.
