## Part A — Prepositions (15 min)

### Time
| Use | Examples |
|-----|----------|
| **at** | at 5 pm, at night, at Christmas |
| **on** | on Monday, on 22 May, on my birthday |
| **in** | in 2026, in May, in the morning, in 2 hours |
| **for** | for 3 years (duration) |
| **since** | since 2020 (starting point) |
| **by** | by 5 pm (deadline) |
| **from…to** | from 9 to 5 |

### Place
| Use | Examples |
|-----|----------|
| **at** | at the door, at home, at the bank |
| **on** | on the table, on the floor |
| **in** | in India, in the room, in the branch |

### Other common
- **between** two · **among** many  
- **depend on** · **arrive at/in** · **good at** · **married to**  
- **according to** · **in spite of** · **because of**

### Conjunctions (join clauses)
| Word | Use |
|------|-----|
| **and, but, or** | join equal ideas |
| **because, since** | reason |
| **although, though** | contrast |
| **if, unless** | condition |
| **when, while, before, after** | time |

**Trap:** *discuss about* ❌ → *discuss* ✓ · *enter into* ❌ → *enter* ✓

## Part B — 15 questions

*Choose best option or find error part.*

1. The meeting is scheduled **(a) in (b) on (c) at (d) by** Friday morning.  
2. She has been working here **(a) since (b) for (c) from (d) by** 2022.  
3. Wait **(a) for (b) to (c) at (d) on** me at the ATM.  
4. The files are **(a) in (b) on (c) at (d) into** the desktop folder.  
5. **(a) Between (b) Among (c) With (d) Of** the three candidates, Raj scored highest.  
6. He is good **(a) in (b) at (c) on (d) for** mathematics.  
7. (A) The manager insisted / (B) for / (C) immediate payment / (D) No error  
8. I will finish the report **(a) until (b) by (c) since (d) for** 6 pm.  
9. **Although / Because / Unless** it was raining, the branch remained open.  
10. She is married **(a) with (b) to (c) by (d) for** an engineer.  
11. (A) He discussed / (B) about the loan / (C) with the customer / (D) No error  
12. The train arrived **(a) in (b) at (c) on (d) to** the station on time.  
13. **(a) In (b) On (c) At (d) During** the exam, do not use your phone.  
14. **(a) And (b) But (c) So (d) Or** he studied hard, he could not clear the cutoff.  
15. Divide the amount equally **(a) between (b) among (c) in (d) to** the four officers.

---

## Part C — Answers

| Q | Ans | Note |
|---|-----|------|
| 1 | b | on + day/date |
| 2 | a | since + point in time |
| 3 | a | wait for |
| 4 | a | in folder |
| 5 | b | among ≥3 |
| 6 | b | good at |
| 7 | B | insisted **on** (not for) |
| 8 | b | by = deadline |
| 9 | Although | contrast |
| 10 | b | married to |
| 11 | B | remove **about** |
| 12 | b | arrive at station |
| 13 | d | During the exam |
| 14 | b | But (contrast) — Although also possible if “though he studied” |
| 15 | b | among four |

**Q14 best:** **(b) But** — *He studied hard, **but** he could not…*
