## Part A — Tenses (read 15 min)

### Simple Present
- Habit / fact: *The bank **opens** at 10.*  
- Subject + V1 (+ s/es for he/she/it)

### Simple Past
- Completed action: *He **submitted** the form yesterday.*  
- Subject + V2

### Simple Future
- *will/shall + V1*: *The results **will be** declared soon.*

### Present Continuous
- Now / temporary: *am/is/are + V-ing*: *She **is processing** the loan.*

### Past Continuous
- Past ongoing: *was/were + V-ing*: *They **were working** when the server failed.*

### Present Perfect
- Past linked to now: *has/have + V3*: *He **has completed** the training.*  
- **Since/for:** *has worked **since** 2020 · **for** 3 years*

### Past Perfect
- Before another past action: *had + V3*: *The branch **had closed** before we arrived.*

### Common errors (exam)
| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| He **have** done | He **has** done |
| I **am working** here since 2020 | I **have worked** here since 2020 |
| Yesterday he **has gone** | Yesterday he **went** |
| If he **will come**, … | If he **comes**, … (Type 1) |

### Sentence improvement tip
- Read all options.  
- Check **tense marker** (yesterday → past; since → present perfect).  
- Check **subject–verb agreement**.

## Part B — 15 sentence improvement / error spotting

*Choose the best replacement for the underlined part OR find the error part (A/B/C/D).*

1. He **work here** since 2019.  
   - (a) works here  (b) has worked here  (c) is working here  (d) worked here  

2. The manager **has approved** the request yesterday.  
   - (a) approves  (b) had approved  (c) approved  (d) has been approving  

3. By the time we reached, the meeting **already started**.  
   - (a) already starts  (b) has already started  (c) had already started  (d) will start  

4. She **is knowing** the password.  
   - (a) knows  (b) is known  (c) has known  (d) knew  

5. If you **will submit** the form, it will be processed.  
   - (a) submit  (b) submitted  (c) have submitted  (d) would submit  

6. (A) The clerks / (B) was working / (C) overtime / (D) last week  

7. I **have seen** him two days ago.  
   - (a) saw  (b) see  (c) had seen  (d) am seeing  

8. The data **were** corrupted last night.  
   - (a) was  (b) are  (c) has been  (d) No change  

9. He **told** that he will come tomorrow.  
   - (a) said  (b) tells  (c) would come  (d) both (a) and (c)  

10. (A) She has been / (B) waiting for / (C) since two hours / (D) No error  

11. **Listen!** Someone **knocked** at the door.  
   - (a) knocks  (b) is knocking  (c) has knocked  (d) was knocking  

12. The bank **launches** a new app next month.  
   - (a) launched  (b) will launch  (c) has launched  (d) launching  

13. (A) Neither of the officers / (B) were present / (C) at the audit / (D) No error  

14. I wish I **studied** harder when I was in college.  
   - (a) study  (b) have studied  (c) had studied  (d) will study  

15. (A) The report / (B) has been submitted / (C) by the IT team / (D) last Friday

## Part C — Answers

| Q | Ans | Why (short) |
|---|-----|-------------|
| 1 | b | since → present perfect |
| 2 | c | yesterday → simple past |
| 3 | c | earlier past → past perfect |
| 4 | a | know = stative, no continuous |
| 5 | a | If clause Type 1 → present |
| 6 | B | clerks → were |
| 7 | a | ago → simple past |
| 8 | a | data = singular mass noun often |
| 9 | d | said + would come |
| 10 | C | since two hours → **for** two hours |
| 11 | b | Listen! → now → continuous |
| 12 | b | next month → future |
| 13 | B | Neither → **was** |
| 14 | c | wish → past perfect |
| 15 | D | conflict: has been vs last Friday → **was submitted** on last Friday — error in B: **was submitted** |

**Q15 best fix:** B → **was submitted** (simple past for last Friday)
